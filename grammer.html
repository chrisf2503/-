    XはYです

    We want to make some of the following statments like "I am a teacher" , "It is 10:30", we can use a noun followed by です(desu).

    Sentence structure: ～ですー＞It is...

  • せんせいです ー＞ (I) am a teacher.
  • じゅうじはんです ー＞ (It) is half past 10.

    • Within the Japanese language, the subject such as "I", "it", or "my (something)", are sometimes omitted, where the speaker would do this if they think that they are being clear to the listener on what they might be referring to.

    But let say that the coversation might not be clear at first, could be about lack of context or lack of background. We can then use the particle 「は」as a topic marker. When reading this, we say "wa" instead of "ha".

    Sentence structure: 「XはYですー＞X is Y. As For X, it is Y 」

  • わたしはやまかわですー＞I am yamagawa
  • わたしはせんせいですー＞I am a teacher
  • ともだちはにほんじんですー＞(My) friend is Japanese
  • せんこうはにほんごですー＞(My) major is Japanese Language

    • This「は」is also known as a particle, we do this in order to show how the phrases relate to the reest of the sentence.
